How it flows

From your voice to a finished result, with a human in the loop only when it matters.

🎙️ Dictate on phone→ n8n→ ClickUp task
Next + agent-ready→ ntfy kick→ Local worker · claude -p→ 🤖 Result comment
Needs your input? The task moves to Waiting and ClickBot @mentions you on every device. Mac asleep and a task waiting? A cloud watchdog pings you, and queued work runs the moment your Mac wakes.

What makes it hold up

The demo is easy. The reliability is the work.

Runs on your plan

The agent executes through your local Claude Code CLI on a Pro or Max subscription. No per-token metering, no surprise bill.

Two-way collaboration

When the agent hits a wall, it asks. You reply in a ClickUp comment and it resumes from your answer, no restart.

Self-healing

A watchdog catches stuck tasks (Mac asleep or worker down) and alerts you once. On wake, queued work runs in seconds.

Safe by default

The worker runs in Claude Code auto mode, where a safety classifier reviews every action, plus a hard deny-list for destructive commands.

Under the hood

Five pieces, each independently testable.

The worker. A stdlib-Python loop that claims Next + agent-ready tasks, runs headless Claude, grades the output against evals, runs a publish-safety check, and writes the result back.

Event-driven wake. A ClickUp webhook to n8n to an ntfy topic your Mac subscribes to over an outbound stream. No inbound port, no tunnel.

Voice capture. An iPhone Shortcut dictates and posts a task through n8n, wired to Back Tap, Siri, or a home-screen icon.

Native notifications. A ClickUp Automation posts as ClickBot so an @mention actually reaches you, sidestepping self-notification suppression.

Watchdog. A cloud schedule flags any task left unworked past 15 minutes and pings you, so nothing silently rots.
